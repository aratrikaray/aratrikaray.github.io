About Me

I am Aratrika Ray-Dowling. On this portfolio website I showcase my fine-arts projects which I have been making since 2009. I am not an artist by profession but I make art projects in my leisure. Making art gives me a sense of freedom to express myself when words fall short and it is also soothes my mind when I feel lost. You will peek into my creative end if you are reading this page. Sometimes I need to be pretty bold in flaunting this passion of my art-making as there are stereotypes which dictates that an engineer cannot be an artist or in other words if they are an artist then in no way they are good engineers! Well, luckily I did not face a person yet who expressed this stereotype on my face but I am aware that this thought process exists. Making art beside my original profession is such a refreshment that I ignore such stereotypes which bind me to a box! So I create whenever I feel like and whenever I can. Art did not just appear to me one day! I got training from professionals since a very tender age. My parents always insisted that I would take interest in different activities beyond my formal education. They had exposed me to different cultural counterparts and art was one of those. There was also an unsaid pressure from school-work that needed students to make scientific diagrams. The better the diagrams are the better the grades will be! The education system in India together with parenting structure usually make children look into different activities beyond studies. When I was around 5-6 years old a time came when those scientific diagrams would haunt me. A lot of my friends already started taking training from art school but I was yet to start. I remember my first scientific diagram was water cycle whose neatness took me a lot of time to grasp. I expressed to my parents that I need to do good in those diagrams. My father taught me that while they search for a good art school for my age I can draw with dots and then later on join them to make the final neat diagram. This way erasing a few dots will be easier and I can section my diagram into smaller pieces. That worked well for a while but later I realized that it was taking a long time!
